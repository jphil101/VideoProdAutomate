What if architects stopped acting like engineers and started acting like biologists? Biomimicry is changing how we build. Take the Eastgate Centre in Zimbabwe. It survives extreme heat with almost zero air conditioning. The secret? It is modeled after a termite mound. Termites build tall structures that naturally vent hot air out the top. This sucks cool air in at the bottom. The Eastgate Centre copies this exact airflow trick. But it gets crazier. Look at the Gherkin skyscraper in London. Its iconic curved shape is not just for looks. It was inspired by the Venus flower basket. That is a deep-sea sponge. The sponge's lattice structure survives crushing ocean currents. By copying this exact geometry, the Gherkin easily deflects massive city winds. This cuts its structural stress in half. Nature has been solving design problems for billions of years. We are just finally taking notes.
