Dry powder is officially dying on construction sites. And honestly? It’s about time.

For decades, we’ve relied on workers mixing drywall fillers by hand, guessing the water ratio by feel. But a little too much water completely changes the shrinking and drying time. The result? Cracked joints, patchy paint, and tons of toxic airborne dust.

This is exactly what happened to concrete in the 1920s. People used to mix it by hand on-site until the industry realized it was too unpredictable and shifted to Ready-Mix. Now, interior walls are having that exact same evolution.

Factory-controlled, ready-mixed compounds are taking over. Because they’re mixed perfectly at the factory, application is up to four times faster than traditional cement fillers. No guesswork, no mixing dust, and drastically fewer callbacks.
